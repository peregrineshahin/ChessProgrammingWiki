 [](http://www.graco.c.u-tokyo.ac.jp/~kaneko/) Tomoyuki Kaneko <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> 
**Tomoyuki Kaneko**,  

a Japanese computer scientist, and associate professor at Graduate School of the [University of Tokyo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Tokyo). His research interests include [machine learning in games](Learning "Learning"), and automated feature construction for [evaluation functions](Evaluation_Function "Evaluation Function") of [general game players](General_Game_Playing "General Game Playing"). He is co-author of the open source [Shogi](Shogi "Shogi") program [GPS Shogi](index.php?title=GPS_Shogi&action=edit&redlink=1 "GPS Shogi (page does not exist)") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, available under [GPL](Free_Software_Foundation#GPL "Free Software Foundation") version 2 or later. In April 2013, GPS Shogi, running on a computer cluster of 700 PCs in the University of Tokyo, [beat](Shogi#GPSShogi "Shogi") Hiroyuki Miura, one of the Top-10 professional Shogi players <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a> <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>.

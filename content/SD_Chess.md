**SD Chess**,  

a chess program developed in 1977 by [Ira Baxter](Ira_Baxter "Ira Baxter") from *Software Dynamics*, written entirely in SD Compiler [Basic](Basic "Basic") on a [6800](6800 "6800") microcomputer, using a [minimax](Minimax "Minimax") [tree](Search_Tree "Search Tree") lookahead scheme <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>. To simplify the [move generation](Move_Generation "Move Generation"), SD Chess stores the chess board in the middle of a [12 by 12 field](Mailbox "Mailbox"), with the borders filled with white pawns <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>. SD Chess competed in the [Microcomputer Chess Tournament](MCCT_1978 "MCCT 1978") of the Second West Coast Computer Faire, March 3–5, 1978. A program called *Chess D* by Ira Baxter was on the Stand-By list of the [WCCC 1983](WCCC_1983 "WCCC 1983") in [New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City) <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>.

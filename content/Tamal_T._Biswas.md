**Tamal Tanu Biswas**,  

a Bangladeshi born electrical engineer, computer scientist and lecturer the CS department at [University of Rochester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Rochester). He holds a Ph.D. in computer science from [University at Buffalo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_at_Buffalo), [Amherst, New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amherst,_New_York), USA under advisor [Kenneth W. Regan](Kenneth_W._Regan "Kenneth W. Regan"). His research includes [psychology](index.php?title=Psychology&action=edit&redlink=1 "Psychology (page does not exist)") and human [decision-making](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision-making). Along with Ken Regan he conducted empirical studies in the domain of chess to compare move preferences of human chess players of various skills with move preferences of strong chess engines, and works on how to incorporate search [depth](Depth "Depth") and therefore time into the decision-making model <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>.

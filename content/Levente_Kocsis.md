 [](https://dms.sztaki.hu/en/people/levente-kocsis) Levente Kocsis <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> 
**Levente Kocsis**,  

a Hungarian computer scientist and researcher in Machine [Learning](Learning "Learning") with interests in [Reinforcement Learning](Reinforcement_Learning "Reinforcement Learning"), [Games](Games "Games") like [Chess](Chess "Chess"), [Go](Go "Go"), [Poker](index.php?title=Poker&action=edit&redlink=1 "Poker (page does not exist)") and [Lines of Action](Lines_of_Action "Lines of Action"), [Search](Search "Search") Control, [Neural Networks](Neural_Networks "Neural Networks") and optimization algorithms for combinatorial problems. He defended his Ph.D thesis *Learning Search Decisions* <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a> in 2003 at [Maastricht University](Maastricht_University "Maastricht University"). 
Levente Kocsis is member of the *Machine Learning Research Group* of the [Hungarian Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Academy_of_Sciences).

In 2006, along with [Csaba Szepesvári](Csaba_Szepesv%C3%A1ri "Csaba Szepesvári"), Levente Kocsis introduced [UCT](UCT "UCT") (Upper Confidence bounds applied to Trees), a new algorithm that applies [bandit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-armed_bandit) ideas to guide [Monte-Carlo planning](Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>. UCT accelerated the Monte-Carlo revolution in computer [Go](Go "Go") <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a> and other domains.

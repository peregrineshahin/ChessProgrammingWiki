**Laurent Sifre**,  

 a French mathematician, computer scientist, and senior research scientist at [Google](index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1 "Google (page does not exist)") [DeepMind](index.php?title=DeepMind&action=edit&redlink=1 "DeepMind (page does not exist)"), involved in the [AlphaGo](index.php?title=AlphaGo&action=edit&redlink=1 "AlphaGo (page does not exist)") and [AlphaZero](AlphaZero "AlphaZero") projects mastering the games of [Go](Go "Go"), [chess](Chess "Chess") and [Shogi](Shogi "Shogi"). He holds a Ph.D. in [applied mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_mathematics) from [École Polytechnique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89cole_Polytechnique) under [Stéphane Mallat](Mathematician#SMallat "Mathematician") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, where his research focused on the design of invariant image representations based on the [scattering transforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inverse_scattering_transform). Applications include [texture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Texture), and [image classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contextual_image_classification) <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>.

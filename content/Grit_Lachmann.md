**Grit Lachmann**,

a German computer scientist, who wrote her Master's thesis at [Chemnitz University of Technology](Chemnitz_University_of_Technology "Chemnitz University of Technology") on the topic of [Knowledge](Knowledge "Knowledge") processing from [Endgame Tablebases](Endgame_Tablebases "Endgame Tablebases")
<a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>.
Her approach performed a [tree search](Search "Search") to classify [positions](Chess_Position "Chess Position") and to merge [nodes](Node "Node") belonging to the same class to reduce the [search tree](Search_Tree "Search Tree").
She implemented the KQNKQ-endgame in [rule-based](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rule-based_system) [Prolog](index.php?title=Prolog&action=edit&redlink=1 "Prolog (page does not exist)").

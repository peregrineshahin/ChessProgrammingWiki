The French program **Joker** was a variation of the [Écume](%C3%89cume "Écume") chess engine by [Marc-François Baudot](Marc-Fran%C3%A7ois_Baudot "Marc-François Baudot") and [Jean-Christophe Weill](Jean-Christophe_Weill "Jean-Christophe Weill"). The idea behind it was "Entropic extension", if the position was not stable enough, an [extension](Extensions "Extensions") of the search was made (the stability was considered as a function of the last move) <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>. The idea came from [Vincent David's](Vincent_David "Vincent David") Ph.D. thesis <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>.


Joker won the [2nd French Computer Chess Championship](index.php?title=FCCC_1993&action=edit&redlink=1 "FCCC 1993 (page does not exist)"). Écume hold the second place. Joker did show some impressive play, also at [Aegon 1994](Aegon_1994 "Aegon 1994"), but the idea was abandoned, the search was too slow (Even if it was coded in [assembly language](Assembly "Assembly")).

**Masakazu Muramatsu**,  

a Japanese mathematician, computer scientist, professor at the Graduate School of Informatics and Engineering, [University of Electro-Communications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Electro-Communications), [Chōfu, Tokyo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%C5%8Dfu,_Tokyo),
as well as head and eponymous of its laboratory. The Masakazu Muramatsu laboratory develops algorithms for [optimization problems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_optimization) in the broad sense, with focus on [nonlinear programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonlinear_programming) (NLP), [convex programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convex_optimization) and in particular [semidefinite programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semidefinite_programming) and [Conic programming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conic_optimization).
Masakazu Muramatsu holds a Ph.D. in 1994 from the [Institute of Statistical Mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institute_of_Statistical_Mathematics) <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>.
Along with [Kunihito Hoki](Kunihito_Hoki "Kunihito Hoki") and others, he also co-authored on [search](Search "Search") topics such as [pruning](Pruning "Pruning") in [Shogi](Shogi "Shogi") and [Monte-Carlo Tree Search](Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search") applied to [Go](Go "Go").

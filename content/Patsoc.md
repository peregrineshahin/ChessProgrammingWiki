**Patsoc**,  

a computer chess program by [Hans Berliner](Hans_Berliner "Hans Berliner") written in [BLISS 36](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BLISS) <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>, which played the [WCCC 1983](WCCC_1983 "WCCC 1983") in [New York](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City), running on a [DEC](Digital_Equipment_Corporation "Digital Equipment Corporation") [PDP-10](PDP-10 "PDP-10") with [36 bit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/36-bit) word length. Patsoc first implemented the evaluation feature of the [crippled majority](Doubled_Pawn#CrippledMajority "Doubled Pawn") with [doubled pawns](Doubled_Pawn "Doubled Pawn") involved <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>. Nevertheless, Patsoc is the acronym of "Plays a terrible sort of Chess" <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>.

**Monte-Carlo Alpha-Beta**, **(MCαβ)**  

is a [Best-First search](Best-First "Best-First") algorithm based on [Monte-Carlo Tree Search](Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search") and a shallow [alpha-beta](Alpha-Beta "Alpha-Beta") [depth-first-search](Depth-First "Depth-First"). It is used in [Lines of Action](Lines_of_Action "Lines of Action") and in conjunction with [UCT](UCT "UCT") (Upper Confidence bounds applied to Trees) also in Chess. It is similar to the the [Best-first minimax search](Best-First_Minimax_Search "Best-First Minimax Search") proposed by [Richard Korf](Richard_Korf "Richard Korf") and [Max Chickering](Max_Chickering "Max Chickering") <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>, one distinction between the two might be that MCTS uses usually win/draw/loss scores and Best-First a score from a heuristic [evaluation](Evaluation "Evaluation") function.

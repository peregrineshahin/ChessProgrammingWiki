**Hans Zellner**,

a German programmer involved in the early development and compression of [endgame tablebases](Endgame_Tablebases "Endgame Tablebases"), making three, four and even five piece [endgames](Endgame "Endgame") such as [KRK](KRK "KRK") (July 1985), KQK (September 1985), [KPK](KPK "KPK") (November 1985), KBBK (July 1986), [KBNK](KBNK_Endgame "KBNK Endgame"), KQKR, and KBBKN available for [home-computers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_computer), the three piece endgames and KBBK already on a [Commodore 64](Commodore_64 "Commodore 64") <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>. He developed a program which perfectly played KQKR to run on an [Atari ST](Atari_ST "Atari ST") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>, a five-piece endgame, KBBKN had to be stored on 59 [floppy disks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floppy_disk) <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>.

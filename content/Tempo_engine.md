**Tempo**,  

an experimental chess engine developed by [Mathieu Autonès](index.php?title=Mathieu_Auton%C3%A8s&action=edit&redlink=1 "Mathieu Autonès (page does not exist)"), [Aryel Beck](index.php?title=Aryel_Beck&action=edit&redlink=1 "Aryel Beck (page does not exist)"), [Phillippe Camacho](index.php?title=Phillippe_Camacho&action=edit&redlink=1 "Phillippe Camacho (page does not exist)"), [Nicolas Lassabe](index.php?title=Nicolas_Lassabe&action=edit&redlink=1 "Nicolas Lassabe (page does not exist)"), [Hervé Luga](index.php?title=Herv%C3%A9_Luga&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hervé Luga (page does not exist)"), and [François Scharffe](index.php?title=Fran%C3%A7ois_Scharffe&action=edit&redlink=1 "François Scharffe (page does not exist)"), using an [artificial neuronal network](Neural_Networks "Neural Networks") as [evaluation function](Evaluation_Function "Evaluation Function") generated by a [genetic algorithm](Genetic_Programming#GeneticAlgorithm "Genetic Programming"). Tempo applies [L-systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L-system) as elaborated by [Egbert Boers](index.php?title=Egbert_Boers&action=edit&redlink=1 "Egbert Boers (page does not exist)") and [Herman Kuiper](index.php?title=Herman_Kuiper&action=edit&redlink=1 "Herman Kuiper (page does not exist)") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, to generate [modular neural networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modular_neural_network) whose size is independent of that of the [chromosome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chromosome_%28genetic_algorithm%29) and [crossover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crossover_%28genetic_algorithm%29) tolerant. A population of L-system construction rules is generated to mark the resulting networks according to their capabilities to [learn](Learning "Learning") game position evaluations from real games. Simple aspects were learnt at first. Strongest individuals learnt more complex faetures, from [material](Material "Material") to [square control](Square_Control "Square Control") and [pawn structure](Pawn_Structure "Pawn Structure") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>.

## Abstract




|  |
| --- |
|  In this article we present our chess engine Tempo. One of the major difficulties for this type of program lies in the function for evaluating game positions. This function is composed of a large number of parameters which have to be determined and then adjusted. We propose an alternative which consists in replacing this function by an artificial neuron network (ANN). Without topological knowledge of this complex network, we use the evolutionist methods for its inception, thus enabling us to obtain, among other things, a modular network. Finally, we present our results:
* Reproduction of the [XOR function](Combinatorial_Logic#XOR "Combinatorial Logic") which [validates](Neural_Networks#AIWinter "Neural Networks") the method used
* Generation of an evaluation function

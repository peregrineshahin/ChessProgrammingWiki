 [](http://www.flickr.com/photos/10261668@N05/859040990/in/set-72157600922172552/) Intelligent Chess <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> 
**Intelligent Chess**, (SciSys Intelligent Chess)  

a [dedicated chess computer](Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") and early forerunner of both [chess database](Databases "Databases") and chess display system based on a system called [Tolinka](index.php?title=Tolinka&action=edit&redlink=1 "Tolinka (page does not exist)") by [Barry Savage](index.php?title=Barry_Savage&action=edit&redlink=1 "Barry Savage (page does not exist)"), a [2D Graphics Board](2D_Graphics_Board "2D Graphics Board") displayed on a [Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television) screen with [flashing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flashing) square features. Further, games along with audio commentaries could be stored and retrieved using an integrated [Stereo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stereophonic_sound) [audio cassette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Audio_cassette) [tape recorder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tape_recorder). Intelligent Chess was developed by [Philidor Software](Philidor_Software "Philidor Software"), and manufactured by [SciSys](Saitek "Saitek") for the associated *Intelligent Games Ltd.*, forerunner of [Intelligent Software](Intelligent_Software "Intelligent Software"), released in 1980.

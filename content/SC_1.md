**Schachcomputer SC 1**,  

[VEB Funkwerk Erfurt's](VEB_Mikroelektronik_Erfurt "VEB Mikroelektronik Erfurt") first [dedicated chess computer](Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") species from 1981, build in low quantities of only about 12 to 20 pieces. The SC 1 was merely a prototype kind of a chess computer, to test the performance of their own [Z80](Z80 "Z80") clone, the [U880](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U880) 8-bit processor <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a> . The program wasn't yet an own development <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>, but a program from the "[Enemy of the people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enemy_of_the_people)", a plain copy of [Ron Nelson's](Ron_Nelson "Ron Nelson") program of a [Chess Challenger](Chess_Challenger "Chess Challenger") manufactured by [Fidelity Electronics](Fidelity_Electronics "Fidelity Electronics") <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>. Remarkably, its successor, the [SC 2](SC_2 "SC 2"), which was produced in higher quantities and exported to the [capitalistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capitalism) "[First World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_World)" to merit foreign exchanges, still was a slightly modified Ron Nelson program <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a> <a id="cite-note-6" href="#cite-ref-6">[6]</a>.

**Aja (Shih-Chieh) Huang**,  

a Taiwanese research scientist at [Google](index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1 "Google (page does not exist)") [DeepMind](index.php?title=DeepMind&action=edit&redlink=1 "DeepMind (page does not exist)"), [6-dan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Go_ranks_and_ratings) [Go](Go "Go") player, co-author of [AlphaGo](index.php?title=AlphaGo&action=edit&redlink=1 "AlphaGo (page does not exist)") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, and computer scientist with a Ph.D. in 2011 on *New Heuristics for Monte Carlo Tree Search Applied to the Game of Go* from [National Taiwan Normal University](National_Taiwan_Normal_University "National Taiwan Normal University"). Aja Huang is author of the Go playing program [AjaGo](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=101), and supported by his supervisor [Rémi Coulom](R%C3%A9mi_Coulom "Rémi Coulom"), author of the world class Go program [Erica](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=530), which won the Gold medal at the [15th Computer Olympiad](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/tournament.php?id=216) in [Kanazawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kanazawa,_Ishikawa), 2010. He is further member of the team in developing the [Chinese Chess](Chinese_Chess "Chinese Chess") program [Elephant](index.php?title=Elephant_(Xiangqi)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Elephant (Xiangqi) (page does not exist)"), and co-author of the [Connect6](Connect6 "Connect6") program [NTNU C6](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=521) <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>.


Aja Huang was postdoc at [University of Alberta](University_of_Alberta "University of Alberta"), researching on [Monte-Carlo Tree Search](Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search"), and working with [Martin Müller](Martin_M%C3%BCller "Martin Müller") on the Go playing programs *Erica* and [Fuego](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=535), as well with [Ryan Hayward](Ryan_Hayward "Ryan Hayward") on the [Hex](Hex "Hex") playing program [MoHex](Ryan_Hayward#MoHex "Ryan Hayward") <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>.

**Ron Hansen**,  

an American computer scientist and early computer chess programmer when he was undergraduate student at [University of Waterloo](University_of_Waterloo "University of Waterloo") in the mid 70s. Along with [Jim Parry](Jim_Parry "Jim Parry"), [Russell Crook](Russell_Crook "Russell Crook"), and [Gary Calnek](Gary_Calnek "Gary Calnek"), he was co-author of the successful mainframe programs [Ribbit](Ribbit "Ribbit") <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> and its successor [Treefrog](Treefrog "Treefrog") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, and wrote a master thesis on computer chess <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>. At his time at Waterloo, [Jonathan Schaeffer](Jonathan_Schaeffer "Jonathan Schaeffer") benefited from the presence of Ron Hansen, who generously gave him a copy of Ribbit's [Fortran](Fortran "Fortran") source code, which he used to learn how to write a chess program <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>.






* Ron Hansen (**1977**). *TREEFROG: a chess program*. Master of Mathematics essay, Computer Science, [University of Waterloo](University_of_Waterloo "University of Waterloo") <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a>

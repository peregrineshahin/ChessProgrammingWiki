**Excalibur King Arthur**,

a [dedicated chess computer](Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") by [Excalibur Electronics](Excalibur_Electronics "Excalibur Electronics"), released in 2000 <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>.
The computer has a [Samsung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsung_Electronics) S3C72P9 controller with a [SAM47](SAM "SAM") 4-bit core at 1.5 MHz with 32 KiB [ROM](Memory#ROM "Memory") and 1056 [nibbles](Nibble "Nibble") of [RAM](Memory#RAM "Memory"),
and features a press [sensory board](Sensory_Board "Sensory Board") along with a [LCD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid-crystal_display) board and button panel.
The 4-bit program, developed by [Ron Nelson](Ron_Nelson "Ron Nelson") during his [sabbatical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabbatical) in late 1999 for the [Excalibur LCD Chess](Excalibur_LCD_Chess "Excalibur LCD Chess") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>,
applies sophisticated [PV move](PV-Move "PV-Move") and [killer move](Killer_Move "Killer Move") [ordering heuristics](Move_Ordering "Move Ordering") <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>,
and was slightly customized for the new hardware.

**Jacek Mańdziuk**,  

a Polish computer scientist, mathematician, and full professor at Faculty of Mathematics and Information Science, [Warsaw University of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warsaw_University_of_Technology) including multiple visiting positions, f.i. at [University of Alberta](University_of_Alberta "University of Alberta") working with [Jonathan Schaeffer](Jonathan_Schaeffer "Jonathan Schaeffer") et al. . He holds a Ph. D. in [Applied mathematics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_Mathematics), Faculty of Applied Physics and Mathematics, Institute of Mathematics, Warsaw University of Technology, 1993 <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, and a [Habilitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habilitation) in Computer science, Systems Research Institute, [Polish Academy of Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_Academy_of_Sciences), [Warsaw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warsaw), 2000. His research interests include the application of [computational intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computational_intelligence) (CI) methods to game playing, [neural networks](Neural_Networks "Neural Networks"), [bioinformatics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioinformatics), [financial modeling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_modeling), [time series prediction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_series) and development of constructive [learning](Learning "Learning") methods for [artificial agents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligent_agent).

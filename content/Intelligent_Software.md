**Intelligent Software**,  

a British computer chess and AI games developing and trading company with focus on [research and development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_and_development) of [dedicated chess computers](Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers"), founded in autumn 1981 by [David Levy](David_Levy "David Levy") and [Kevin O’Connell](Kevin_O%E2%80%99Connell "Kevin O’Connell"). Intelligent Software evolved from their former company [Philidor Software](Philidor_Software "Philidor Software"), initially as a separate company to no longer develop programs exclusively for [SciSys](Saitek "Saitek"), but also for [Milton Bradley](Milton_Bradley "Milton Bradley"), [Newcrest Technology](Newcrest_Technology "Newcrest Technology"), and others <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a> . The new company name was already advised by *Intelligent Games Ltd.*, distributing [Intelligent Chess](Intelligent_Chess "Intelligent Chess") since 1980. Intelligent Software had several computer chess programmers under contract. The programmers already affiliated with Philidor Software, [Mark Taylor](Mark_Taylor "Mark Taylor"), [David Broughton](David_Broughton "David Broughton"), and [Mike Johnson](Mike_Johnson "Mike Johnson"), were supplemented by [Richard Lang](Richard_Lang "Richard Lang") and a [Checkers](Checkers "Checkers") programmer called [Martin Bryant](Martin_Bryant "Martin Bryant") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a> .

In late 1982, Intelligent Software was commissioned to develop a [home computer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_computer), the [Z80](Z80 "Z80") based [Enterprise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enterprise_%28computer%29) with 64 resp. 128 KiB of [bank switched](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_switching) [RAM](Memory#RAM "Memory"), and a [Basic](Basic "Basic") interpreter in [ROM](Memory#ROM "Memory"). Announced in September 1983 with a advised release date of April 1984, the Enterprise did not ship until 1985 <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a> . In Germany, the computer was distributed by [Hegener & Glaser](Hegener_%26_Glaser "Hegener & Glaser") as *Mephisto PHC 64*.



## Close Down


Intelligent Software was closed down in 1986 when its biggest customer, the commissioning company defaulted on a very large payment <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a> .

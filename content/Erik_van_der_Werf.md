[](https://github.com/evdwerf) Erik van der Werf <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>
**Erik van der Werf**,

a Dutch computer scientist, principal scientist in the [hearing aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hearing_aid) industry, and master level [Go](Go "Go") player <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>.
He received a M.Sc. degree in [applied physics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applied_physics) from [Delft University of Technology](Delft_University_of_Technology "Delft University of Technology") in 1999, and defended his Ph.D. thesis on [artificial intelligence](Artificial_Intelligence "Artificial Intelligence") for the game of [Go](Go "Go") at [Maastricht University](Maastricht_University "Maastricht University") in 2005 under supervision of [Jaap van den Herik](Jaap_van_den_Herik "Jaap van den Herik") and [Jos Uiterwijk](Jos_Uiterwijk "Jos Uiterwijk").
Erik van der Werf is author of the [Go](Go "Go") playing programs [Magog](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=137) (started with [Levente Kocsis](Levente_Kocsis "Levente Kocsis") and [Mark Winands](Mark_Winands "Mark Winands")) <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>, [Steenvreter](https://www.game-ai-forum.org/icga-tournaments/program.php?id=519) <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a> and GridMaster <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a>.
His Mini Go Solver *Migos* solved 5x5 Go in 2002 <a id="cite-note-6" href="#cite-ref-6">[6]</a>,
using [iterative deepening](Iterative_Deepening "Iterative Deepening") [alpha-beta](Alpha-Beta "Alpha-Beta") search ([PVS](Principal_Variation_Search "Principal Variation Search")), with [transposition tables](Transposition_Table "Transposition Table"), [enhanced transposition cut-offs](Enhanced_Transposition_Cutoff "Enhanced Transposition Cutoff"),
two [killer moves](Killer_Move "Killer Move"), [history heuristic](History_Heuristic "History Heuristic"), Benson's algorithm for unconditional [live](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_and_death) <a id="cite-note-7" href="#cite-ref-7">[7]</a> <a id="cite-note-8" href="#cite-ref-8">[8]</a>, and domain specific evaluation heuristics, futher elaborated in his Ph.D. thesis and [ICGA Journal](ICGA_Journal "ICGA Journal") papers.

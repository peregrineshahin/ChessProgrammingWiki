**Andre Adrian**,

a German senior engineer with background in [telecommunication](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecommunication), [real-time processing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real-time_computing) and [air traffic control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_traffic_control). His interests include computer chess and computer chess history, he maintains a computer chess site in German, covering [El Ajedrecista](El_Ajedrecista "El Ajedrecista"), [Turochamp](Turochamp "Turochamp"), [Dietrich Prinz'](Dietrich_Prinz "Dietrich Prinz") [Mate-in-two](Mate-in-two "Mate-in-two"), and early chess programs and [Dedicated Chess Computers](Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>. Andre Adrian further ported the [Sargon](Sargon "Sargon") [Z80](Z80 "Z80") [assembly](Assembly "Assembly") program by [Kathe](Kathe_Spracklen "Kathe Spracklen") and [Dan Spracklen](Dan_Spracklen "Dan Spracklen") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a> to run under the [CP/M](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CP/M) [operating system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_system), and [Harm Geert Muller's](Harm_Geert_Muller "Harm Geert Muller") [Micro-Max](Micro-Max "Micro-Max") for an [Atmel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atmel)-[ATmega88](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ATmega88) [Microcontroller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microcontroller) <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a> using [WinAVR GCC](http://winavr.sourceforge.net/), deployed for a self-made [chess computer](Dedicated_Chess_Computers "Dedicated Chess Computers") *SHAH* <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a> and the *ATM18 mini chess computer* <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a> from the electronics magazine [Elektor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elektor).

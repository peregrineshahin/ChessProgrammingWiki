**Ilya Sutskever**,  

 a Canadian computer scientist, and co-founder and research director of [OpenAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OpenAI), a non-profit AI research company. Before, he was research scientist at [Google](index.php?title=Google&action=edit&redlink=1 "Google (page does not exist)") [Brain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Brain), and co-founded [DNNresearch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_mergers_and_acquisitions_by_Google) <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>. He was postdoc at [Stanford University](Stanford_University "Stanford University") with [Andrew Ng's](index.php?title=Andrew_Ng&action=edit&redlink=1 "Andrew Ng (page does not exist)") group, and received his M.Sc. in 2007 <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a> and his Ph.D. in 2013 <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a> from [University of Toronto](University_of_Toronto "University of Toronto"), working with [Geoffrey Hinton](Mathematician#GEHinton "Mathematician"). His research interests include [machine learning](Learning "Learning") and [neural networks](Neural_Networks "Neural Networks"), in particular [deep learning](Deep_Learning "Deep Learning") with [convolutional neural networks](Neural_Networks#Convolutional "Neural Networks") <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a> applied to [machine translation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_translation), [voice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Speech_recognition) and [image recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_vision#Recognition), and also to the game of [Go](Go#CNN "Go") <a id="cite-note-6" href="#cite-ref-6">[6]</a> .

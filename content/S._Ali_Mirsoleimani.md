 [](https://www.linkedin.com/in/alimirsoleimani/) S. Ali Mirsoleimani <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> 
**Sayyed Ali Mirsoleimani**,  

an Iranian computer scientist and Ph.D. candidate at the [Leiden Centre of Data Science](Leiden_University#LCDS "Leiden University") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>, [Leiden University](Leiden_University "Leiden University") and [Nikhef](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikhef), [Amsterdam Science Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam_Science_Park).
He holds a M.Sc. degree in computer software engineering from [Shiraz University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shiraz_University), [Shiraz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shiraz) in 2013, and a B.Sc. from [Islamic Azad University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Azad_University) in 2010. 
His research interests include [machine learning](Learning "Learning"), [genetic](Genetic_Programming#GeneticAlgorithm "Genetic Programming") and [memetic algorithms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memetic_algorithm), massive parallel high performance computing using [GPU's](GPU "GPU") and the [Xeon Phi](X86-64 "X86-64") architecture. 
Involved in the [HEPGAME](Jos_Vermaseren#HEPGAME "Jos Vermaseren") project headed by [Jos Vermaseren](Jos_Vermaseren "Jos Vermaseren") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>, he works on [Monte-Carlo Tree Search](Monte-Carlo_Tree_Search "Monte-Carlo Tree Search"), [UCT](UCT "UCT"), and [parallel MCTS](Parallel_Search "Parallel Search").

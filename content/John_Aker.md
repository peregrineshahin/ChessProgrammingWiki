 [](https://archive.org/stream/PersonalComputing198102/Personal%20Computing%201981-02#page/n90/mode/1up) John Aker <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a> 
**John L. Aker**,  

an American electrical engineer and consultant affiliated with [Applied Concepts](Applied_Concepts "Applied Concepts"). Along with Applied Concepts [CEO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive_officer) [Alan Mead](Alan_Mead "Alan Mead") and others, he holds various patents <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a> <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a> concerning [Doppler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doppler_radar) complex [FFT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_Fourier_transform) [police radar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radar_gun), which is now the primary business of Applied Concepts <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>. In the '80s, Aker started programming chess, to continue the work on [Boris 2.5](Boris "Boris") based on [Sargon 2.5](Sargon "Sargon") by [Kathe](Kathe_Spracklen "Kathe Spracklen") and [Dan Spracklen](Dan_Spracklen "Dan Spracklen"). The intermediate [Boris Experimental](Boris#X "Boris") (Boris X), runner up of the [WMCCC 1980](WMCCC_1980 "WMCCC 1980"), and forerunner of the [Morphy](Morphy "Morphy") program was denied to participate at the [ACM 1980](ACM_1980 "ACM 1980") after a protest filed by Kathe Spracklen causing a [controversy](Boris#Controversy "Boris") <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a>

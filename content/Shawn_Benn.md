**Shawn Benn**,  

an American computer scientist, software and database engineer, from 1989 until 1996 affiliated with the Department of Computer Science, [University of Maine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Maine), and since then, holding various positions in the industry <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>. In 1992, along with [Danny Kopec](Danny_Kopec "Danny Kopec"), he recalibrated the [Bratko-Kopec Test](Bratko-Kopec_Test "Bratko-Kopec Test") using six commercial chess programs, 
[Chessmaster 3000](Chessmaster#3000 "Chessmaster"), [Excel 68000](Excel "Excel"), [MChess](MChess "MChess"), [Sargon IV](Sargon "Sargon"), [Sargon V](Sargon "Sargon") and [Zarkov 2.5](Zarkov "Zarkov") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>.

**Lex Weaver**,  

an Australian computer scientist, since 2004 manager at the [Australian Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Australia), and before lecturer in the Department of Computer Science at the [Australian National University](Australian_National_University "Australian National University"), [Canberra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canberra), Australia, where he already defended his B.Sc. and Ph.D. degrees in 1994 and 2003 respectively <a id="cite-note-1" href="#cite-ref-1">[1]</a>. Lex Weaver researched on [machine learning](Learning "Learning") and in particular [Temporal Difference Learning](Temporal_Difference_Learning "Temporal Difference Learning"), and co-authored of the chess program [KnightCap](KnightCap "KnightCap") along with [Jonathan Baxter](Jonathan_Baxter "Jonathan Baxter") and [Andrew Tridgell](Andrew_Tridgell "Andrew Tridgell") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a>.

**Shogi** (Japanese Chess),  

a chess variant that evolved directly from [Shatranj](Shatranj "Shatranj"), just like the western [chess](Chess "Chess"). It is played on 9x9 board. Compared to chess, Shogi pieces have limited mobility, but this is compensated by the fact that captured enemy pieces can be [dropped](index.php?title=Piece_Drop&action=edit&redlink=1 "Piece Drop (page does not exist)") on the board as one's own. This leads to the wild, tactical game. Shogi has greater [branching factor](Branching_Factor "Branching Factor") than chess. Development of Shogi programs has taken slightly different route than in chess programming. The stress is on [pattern recognition](Pattern_Recognition "Pattern Recognition") and [selective search](Selectivity "Selectivity") techniques. However, with the advent of [NNUE](NNUE "NNUE") <a id="cite-note-2" href="#cite-ref-2">[2]</a> along with adaptations of [Stockfish](Stockfish "Stockfish") to Shogi such as [YaneuraOu](YaneuraOu "YaneuraOu") <a id="cite-note-3" href="#cite-ref-3">[3]</a>, and [Kristallweizen](Kristallweizen "Kristallweizen") <a id="cite-note-4" href="#cite-ref-4">[4]</a>, and the consequent [Stockfish NNUE](Stockfish_NNUE "Stockfish NNUE") hype <a id="cite-note-5" href="#cite-ref-5">[5]</a>, both worlds seem to reunite again.

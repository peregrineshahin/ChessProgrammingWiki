**Playing Strength**, (Performance, Skill Level)  

of a chess player, or chess playing entity, program or engine, reflects the ability to win against other players, given by a number or other element of an [ordered set](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_theory) such as an [Elo number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elo_rating_system). 


The ability to solve [test-positions](Test-Positions "Test-Positions"), that is, finding the specified, likely one and only best move, might be an indicator for various particular engine skills, but does not necessarily correlate with playing strength. In his *[Parallelism and Selectivity in Game Tree Search](Tord_Romstad#Video "Tord Romstad")* lecture, [Tord Romstad](Tord_Romstad "Tord Romstad") introduced the *Worst Moves Observation* (**WMO**), which states the practical playing strength is not primarily determined by the quality of the players best moves nor average moves, but by the quality of the players worst moves. 



## Strength


The strength of a chess program depends on many things, the quality and efficiency of the [algorithms](Algorithms "Algorithms") involved to determine the best move of a position, the balance of the so called [search](Search "Search") versus [knowledge tradeoff](Knowledge "Knowledge") to [evaluate](Evaluation "Evaluation") or compare [leaf nodes](Leaf_Node "Leaf Node") of a [search tree](Search_Tree "Search Tree"), how to shape that tree and to propagate a [score](Score "Score") up to the [root](Root "Root"), and [time management](Time_Management "Time Management"), that is how to allocate time for searching a move under time control requirements. Time used is roughly proportional to the number of visited [nodes](Node "Node") of the common [depth-first search](Depth-First "Depth-First") inside an [iterative deepening frame](Iterative_Deepening "Iterative Deepening"), which [grows exponentially](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exponential_growth) by its [effective branching factor](Branching_Factor#EffectiveBranchingFactor "Branching Factor") raised to the power of [search depth](Depth "Depth"). Playing strength might be improved over the (playing) time due to [learning algorithms](Learning "Learning").
